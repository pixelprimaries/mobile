DISTRICT 13

Illinois's 13th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district covers central and southwestern portions of the state, including parts of Champaign, Macon, Madison, Sangamon, and St. Clair counties. It includes cities such as Champaign, Urbana, Decatur, Springfield (the state capital), and parts of the Metro-East suburbs near St. Louis.

Historically, the 13th District has experienced significant boundary changes due to redistricting, reflecting shifts in population and political alignments. The district was traditionally competitive, with representation frequently alternating between Republicans and Democrats. The district includes a mix of urban centers, university communities—most notably the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign—and agricultural and manufacturing regions. For much of its recent history, the district leaned Republican, represented by Rodney Davis from 2013 until 2023. Davis, a moderate Republican, frequently faced competitive elections due to the district's politically balanced nature. However, following the 2022 redistricting, the district became more favorable to Democrats. Democrat Nikki Budzinski won the seat in 2022, shifting the district's political orientation. Budzinski has focused on issues related to labor, economic development, infrastructure, and healthcare. The district currently has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of D+3, indicating a slight Democratic lean. Its population is predominantly White, with notable Black, Hispanic, and Asian communities primarily concentrated in urban areas. Economically, the district includes agricultural areas, educational institutions, state government offices, and various industries ranging from manufacturing to healthcare. Median household income is close to the national average.
