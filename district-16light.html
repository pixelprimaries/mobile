DISTRICT 16

Illinois's 16th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district encompasses parts of north-central and northwestern Illinois, including portions of Boone, Bureau, DeKalb, Grundy, Jo Daviess, LaSalle, Lee, Ogle, Putnam, Stephenson, Whiteside, and Winnebago counties. Major cities and towns within the district include Rockford, DeKalb, Freeport, Ottawa, LaSalle, Peru, and Morris.

Historically, the 16th District has undergone various changes due to redistricting, reflecting shifting populations and regional alignments. It is most famously associated with former President Ronald Reagan, who represented the district from 1947 to 1953. The district has traditionally leaned Republican, reflecting the conservative and agricultural character of its communities. Since 2013, Republican Adam Kinzinger has represented the district. Kinzinger, a former Air Force pilot, is known nationally for his moderate stances and criticism of elements within his own party. He has prioritized issues related to national security, veterans' affairs, agriculture, and infrastructure development. The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of R+10, indicating a moderate Republican lean. The population is predominantly White, with smaller Hispanic, Black, and Asian communities mainly concentrated in urban areas such as Rockford. Economically, the district includes a combination of agricultural areas, manufacturing towns, and suburban communities. Median household income in the district is slightly below the national average.
