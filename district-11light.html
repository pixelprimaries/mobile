DISTRICT 11

Illinois's 11th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district encompasses parts of Cook, DuPage, Kane, Kendall, and Will counties, including cities such as Aurora, Naperville, Joliet, Bolingbrook, and Romeoville.

The district has undergone multiple changes due to redistricting, with its boundaries shifting significantly over the decades. Historically, it has included both urban and suburban areas, contributing to its political competitiveness. The district is home to a diverse economic base, including industries such as manufacturing, healthcare, technology, and logistics. Aurora, the second-largest city in Illinois, serves as a major population and economic center within the district. The district has alternated between Republican and Democratic representation. It was previously held by Republican Jerry Weller from 1995 to 2009, followed by Democrat Debbie Halvorson for a single term. After Republican Adam Kinzinger defeated Halvorson in 2010, the district was redrawn, and in 2012, Democrat Bill Foster won the seat. Foster, a physicist and businessman, had previously represented the 14th Congressional District. He has since been re-elected multiple times and has focused on economic policy, scientific research funding, and healthcare. The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of D+5, indicating a slight Democratic lean. The population is diverse, with a mix of White, Hispanic, Asian, and Black residents. The median household income is above the national average, reflecting a mix of middle-class and affluent communities. The district also has high education levels, with many residents working in professional and technical fields.
