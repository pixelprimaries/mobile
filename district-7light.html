DISTRICT 7

The 7th congressional district of Illinois covers parts of Cook County, as of the 2023 redistricting that followed the 2020 census. All or parts of Broadview, Bellwood, Chicago, Forest Park, Hillside, Oak Park, La Grange Park, Maywood, and Westchester are included.

Historically, the district has been a hub of cultural and social movements. The Bronzeville neighborhood, often referred to as the "Black Metropolis," became a center for African-American culture and business during the early 20th century, especially during the Great Migration. Additionally, the Oscar Stanton De Priest House, located within the district, was home to the first African-American elected to Congress from a northern state.​ The district is home to several notable cultural institutions. The Adler Planetarium and Astronomy Museum, established in 1930, is recognized as the first planetarium in the Western Hemisphere. The Art Institute of Chicago, founded in 1879, stands as one of the oldest and largest art museums in the United States. Furthermore, the district includes the Ridgeland–Oak Park Historic District, showcasing a variety of architectural styles, including Prairie School and Victorian designs.
