DISTRICT 10

Illinois's 10th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district is located in the northern suburbs of Chicago and includes parts of Cook, Lake, and McHenry counties. It encompasses cities and towns such as Waukegan, North Chicago, Highland Park, Lake Forest, Buffalo Grove, and parts of Arlington Heights.

The district has undergone several changes due to redistricting, reflecting population shifts in the Chicago metropolitan area. Historically, it was a Republican stronghold, but in recent decades, it has leaned Democratic. It includes a mix of affluent suburban communities, working-class neighborhoods, and diverse populations. The district also contains major economic hubs, with industries ranging from manufacturing to finance and healthcare. The district has alternated between Republican and Democratic representation. For many years, it was represented by Republican Mark Kirk until he won a U.S. Senate seat in 2010. In 2012, Democrat Brad Schneider won the seat, only to lose it in 2014 to Republican Bob Dold. In 2016, Schneider reclaimed the seat and has held it since. He has focused on issues such as healthcare, environmental protection, and economic development. The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of D+11, indicating a Democratic lean. The population is diverse, with a significant White majority alongside growing Hispanic, Asian, and Black communities. The median household income is above the national average, reflecting the presence of both affluent suburbs and middle-class neighborhoods. The district also has high education levels, with many residents holding college degrees.
