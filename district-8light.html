DISTRICT 8

​Illinois's 8th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district covers parts of Cook, DuPage, and Kane counties, including cities such as Elgin, Schaumburg, and portions of Chicago's northwest suburbs.

The 8th Congressional District has undergone several changes due to redistricting over the years. Historically, it has encompassed different regions within Illinois, reflecting population shifts and political considerations. In its current configuration, the district includes a diverse population with a mix of urban and suburban communities.​ Politically, the district has experienced shifts between Republican and Democratic representation. Notably, Republican Philip M. Crane represented the district for several decades until 2004. In 2004, Democrat Melissa Bean defeated Crane, signaling a shift in the district's political landscape. Bean served until 2010, after which Republican Joe Walsh held the seat for one term. In 2012, Democrat Tammy Duckworth won the seat and served until 2016. Since 2017, Democrat Raja Krishnamoorthi has represented the district. Krishnamoorthi has been re-elected in subsequent elections, including the 2022 general election, where he defeated Republican challenger Chris Dargis with 56.9% of the vote.​ The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of D+6, indicating a Democratic lean. The demographic composition is diverse, with approximately 54.4% White, 23.8% Hispanic, 14.3% Asian, and 4.3% Black populations. The median household income is $94,702, and the district is entirely urban.​
